**Design Movement:** Texas Vernacular Modernism — the intersection of raw Hill Country materials and clean contemporary architecture.

**Core Principles:**
1. Warm materiality: limestone, cedar, and iron textures woven into the UI through subtle background patterns and border treatments.
2. Asymmetric editorial layouts — full-bleed imagery on one side, typographic content on the other, never centered grids.
3. Generous negative space that mirrors the expansiveness of the Texas landscape.
4. Every section transitions through a warm-toned gradient wash rather than hard dividers.

**Color Philosophy:** The palette is anchored in the land itself — Sandstone White (#F5F0E8), Deep Charcoal (#1C1917), Terracotta (#C2714F), and Hill Country Sage (#6B7C5C). Terracotta is the signature accent, used sparingly for CTAs and highlights to evoke the warmth of a Texas sunset without feeling rustic.

**Layout Paradigm:** Horizontal split-screen sections alternate between image-dominant and text-dominant halves. The homepage hero is a full-viewport cinematic image with a bold typographic overlay anchored to the bottom-left. Sections "breathe" with 120px+ vertical padding.

**Signature Elements:**
1. A thin horizontal rule in Terracotta that appears as a section separator, echoing the horizon line of the Texas Hill Country.
2. Large, bold serif numerals (01, 02, 03) used as section counters to give the layout an editorial, magazine-like quality.
3. Subtle limestone-texture SVG backgrounds on alternating sections.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions are deliberate and unhurried — like the pace of craftsmanship. Hover states on cards reveal a slow-fade image overlay with a terracotta tint. Navigation links underline from left to right on hover.

**Animation:** Entrance animations use a gentle upward fade (translateY 20px → 0, opacity 0 → 1, 600ms ease-out). No bouncy or elastic animations. Parallax scrolling on the hero image at 0.4x speed. Section headers slide in from the left.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (bold, italic for emphasis) — conveys heritage and craftsmanship.
- Body: DM Sans (regular, 400/500) — clean and highly readable on mobile.
- Accent: DM Mono (small caps) for labels, stats, and category tags.

**Design Movement:** Quiet Luxury Regionalism — the understated elegance of high-end custom homes expressed through restraint and precision.

**Core Principles:**
1. Restraint as a luxury signal — minimal elements, maximum impact.
2. Photography as the primary design element — the UI exists to frame the homes, not compete with them.
3. Typographic refinement over decorative flourishes.
4. A spatial rhythm based on the golden ratio applied to section proportions.

**Color Philosophy:** Ivory (#FDFAF5), Warm Stone (#8C7B6B), Deep Forest (#2D3B2E), and Aged Brass (#B8924A). The palette is intentionally muted to ensure photography always commands attention. Aged Brass is used only for the logo, key CTAs, and hover states.

**Layout Paradigm:** A magazine-editorial structure with a 12-column grid. The hero uses a 70/30 split: 70% photography, 30% typographic content panel with a semi-transparent warm stone background. Portfolio items use a masonry layout with varying aspect ratios.

**Signature Elements:**
1. A thin Aged Brass border line that frames the logo and appears as a decorative rule in section headers.
2. Oversized, light-weight serif letterforms used as decorative background elements (e.g., a large "R" watermark behind the About section).
3. Photography with a subtle warm film grain overlay to add texture and depth.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Whisper-quiet. Hover states are subtle — a 2% scale increase on portfolio cards, a gentle opacity shift on navigation links. Nothing shouts; everything invites.

**Animation:** Fade-in with a very slight upward drift (8px, 800ms, cubic-bezier ease). Staggered entrance for grid items (100ms delay between each). The hero text fades in word by word.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Cormorant Garamond (light, 300 weight, generous letter-spacing) — the epitome of quiet luxury.
- Body: Lato (regular, 400) — neutral and highly legible.
- Navigation: Lato (500, wide letter-spacing, small caps) — refined and architectural.

## Selected Design: Texas Vernacular Modernism (Response 1)

This approach best captures the RosaTexHomes brand — it is professional and premium without being cold, and it roots the visual identity firmly in the Central Texas landscape. The terracotta accent color and asymmetric editorial layouts will create a distinctive, memorable site that stands apart from generic home builder websites.
